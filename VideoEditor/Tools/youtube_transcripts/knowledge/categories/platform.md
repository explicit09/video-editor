# Platform Knowledge

## Rules

### Rule: Aspect Ratio Affects Pacing
- **Applies to:** All platforms (platform-dependent implementation)
- **Rule:** Vertical formats require tighter pacing and composition; horizontal YouTube allows more breathing room. Reverse aspect ratio from 1920x1080 to 1080x1920 for vertical content.
- **Reasoning:** Screen real estate is fundamentally different between formats. Vertical video requires viewers to process information in a narrower frame, which necessitates tighter editing and composition to maintain engagement.

### Rule: Format-Specific Reediting Required
- **Applies to:** All platforms
- **Rule:** Same content edited for vertical vs. horizontal requires rethinking pacing, headroom, and framing. Do not simply crop 16:9 footage—perform vertical-specific shooting or composition adjustments.
- **Reasoning:** Direct cropping loses compositional intent and creates awkward framing. Intentional vertical editing preserves visual hierarchy and maintains subject focus.

### Rule: Hook in First 3 Seconds
- **Applies to:** All social platforms
- **Rule:** All platforms benefit from hooks in the first 3 seconds to capture attention before viewers scroll past.
- **Reasoning:** Social media algorithms and viewer behavior prioritize immediate engagement. A fast hook determines whether audiences continue watching or disengage.

### Rule: YouTube Long-Form Intro Strategy
- **Applies to:** YouTube (long-form content)
- **Rule:** YouTube long-form content demands a critical intro/hook that gets to the point quickly. Allow for slightly longer intros than Shorts, but hook must be clear within first seconds.
- **Reasoning:** YouTube viewers expect substantive content but will leave if the value proposition isn't immediately clear. Longer form allows narrative setup, but not padding.

### Rule: Music Fill Limit
- **Applies to:** YouTube
- **Rule:** Don't let music run more than 20-30 seconds or it feels like filler. Break up long-form content with title cards and graphics.
- **Reasoning:** Extended music-only segments without visual progression feel like dead space and reduce perceived production value and pacing momentum.

### Rule: Captions Are Essential for Vertical
- **Applies to:** Shorts, Reels, TikTok
- **Rule:** Captions are essential for vertical platform viewing—80%+ viewers watch without sound. Use "karaoke" style word-by-word captions for optimal engagement.
- **Reasoning:** Mobile viewing in public spaces, notifications, and multitasking habits mean most viewers watch muted. Captions become the primary information carrier; word-by-word styling increases comprehension and engagement.

### Rule: Sound Design Importance Scales with Format
- **Applies to:** Shorts/vertical platforms
- **Rule:** Music and sound design become more important as visual context is reduced in vertical formats. Loudness targets: -16 LUFS for podcasts, -14 LUFS for social clips.
- **Reasoning:** With limited visual information in vertical framing, audio becomes a critical engagement tool. Proper loudness ensures consistency and professional presentation across platforms.

### Rule: Platform-Specific Duration Limits
- **Applies to:** Platform-specific
- **Rule:** Respect platform maximums: YouTube Shorts 59s max, TikTok 60s max, Instagram Reels 30s max. Adjust pacing and content structure accordingly.
- **Reasoning:** Platform constraints are hard limits. Structure content to work within these constraints rather than fighting them. Tighter pacing naturally fits shorter formats.

### Rule: Vertical Pacing Tightness
- **Applies to:** Shorts/vertical platforms
- **Rule:** Pacing can be slightly tighter in vertical because screen real estate is limited. Remove silence longer than 0.3 seconds and adjust speech rate: if WPM < 140, apply 1.15x speed adjustment.
- **Reasoning:** Smaller visual frame and mobile viewing context allow faster cuts without feeling chaotic. Continuous stimulation is expected in short-form vertical content.

### Rule: Trend Participation and Audio Syncing
- **Applies to:** TikTok
- **Rule:** TikTok values trend participation and audio syncing. Align visual beats with trending audio and participate in platform-specific challenges.
- **Reasoning:** TikTok's algorithm heavily favors trend engagement and audio-visual synchronization. Participating in trends increases discoverability and audience connection.

### Rule: Muted Viewing Expectation
- **Applies to:** Social platforms
- **Rule:** Assume 80%+ viewers watch without sound. Design all content to communicate clearly without relying on audio as the primary information source.
- **Reasoning:** Platform usage patterns show most viewers consume in muted, public contexts. Visual storytelling must be self-sufficient; audio enhances but doesn't replace.

## Tips

1. **Test aspect ratios early** - When adapting content for new platforms, check framing and pacing on actual mobile devices before finalizing. Screen size and viewing angle change perception significantly.

2. **Batch convert with intentionality** - If converting existing 16:9 content to 9:16, use auto-reframe tools as a starting point, then manually adjust for proper headroom and composition rather than accepting defaults.

3. **Leverage captions as design** - Don't treat captions as plain text. Use color, timing, animation, and positioning to make them part of the visual design and maintain engagement.

4. **Create platform-specific B-roll** - When shooting content for multiple platforms, capture extra material that works specifically for vertical framing (closer shots, vertical movement, face-forward moments).

5. **Monitor loudness across exports** - Different platforms loudness-normalize differently. Export and check actual playback on each platform before publishing to ensure consistent audio levels.
